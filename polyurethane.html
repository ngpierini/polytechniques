How this works

The classic two-step (prepolymer) polyurethane synthesis: first, cap a hydroxyl-terminated polyol with an excess of diisocyanate so every chain end becomes an isocyanate (the NCO:OH ratio, typically 1.6–2.2:1, sets how much free NCO remains). Second, chain extend the NCO-terminated prepolymer with a short diol or diamine, which builds the hard segments and drives molecular weight up. Everything here is equivalents arithmetic:

Polyol eq wt = 56100 ÷ OH number   |   Diisocyanate eq wt = 4202 ÷ %NCO   |   %NCOprepolymer = 4202 × (eq NCO − eq OH) ÷ total mass

In practice you should titrate the real %NCO of your prepolymer (dibutylamine back-titration, ASTM D2572) before extending – side reactions, moisture, and polyol batch variation all pull the real value below theory. Step 2 accepts your measured value for exactly that reason.
